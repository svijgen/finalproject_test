Health and Mental Services

First Place has an on-site Nurse Practictioner, School Psychologist and other mental health professionals. Children receive health screenings free of charge, and our staff helps to ensure that children are up to date with their vaccinations will make referrals as needed for other health needs.
We also take the mental health of all of our clients very seriously. Trauma is a major issue affecting the children of families who are enrolled at First Place. Trauma affects both the physical and mental health of children and happens through an experience that is emotionally painful, distressful, and or shocking.
On-site mental health services are provided for all First Place children and their families hrough a partnership with Antioch University Seattle's clinical doctorate in psychology program. The needs of each child are assessed as they enter school and a plan is designed in partnership with parents and staff to meet those needs. Strategies include play therapy for the child, groupd therapy for parents and children, structural family therapy, cognitive behavioral family therapy, and the use of community based mental health providers.
